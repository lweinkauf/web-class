What is a Website Anyway?

Journal Entry 1 - Lena Weinkauf


From my knowledge of technology prior to this course and from what I have learned in the last few weeks, a website is a hub for an idea to live, breathe, and expand to human beings.

Like a book, a website stores information, information that can be beneficial to people, harmful to people, add value or take it away. Websites get people to think differently, and I would argue, more creatively. They offer the space to distribute knowledge in a methodology that is specifically curtailed to the way our minds work, what we pay most attention to, and what captures our interests the most.

The strength of use of the internet comes from the way we are able to design websites. Unlike books that are organized by chapters, and have a more common structure to order information and tell stories, the internet has created unique and innovational ways to tell stories and to alter the fundamentals of how we seek information and pass it on to others.

Maybe one day the internet won't have the same importance or impact that it does today.

As technology continues to grow and shift with the world, I am sure websites will become an outdated concept, and we will have new means of acquiring and distributing ideas and information. The negatives I feel from the internet as someone who grew up with major shifts in technology is the fact that it is supposed to make people more connected, but it seems to have done the exact opposite.
